1. Purpose

A production system is only as good as its ability to explain itself.

When something goes wrong, engineers should never ask:

"What happened?"

Instead they should ask:

"Where exactly did it happen?"

Observability exists to answer:

What happened?
Why did it happen?
Who caused it?
When did it happen?
Which systems were involved?
Can it happen again?

Every production-grade backend must be observable.

34. Definition of an Observable System

A production-ready Strus backend should:

Explain every request from start to finish.
Measure both technical and business health.
Detect failures before users report them.
Preserve immutable audit history.
Enable engineers to diagnose incidents quickly.
Produce actionable alerts instead of noise.
Continuously improve through postmortems and operational reviews.
Summary

The observability architecture of Strus ensures that every request, transaction, event, and business action can be traced, measured, and audited. Structured logs, distributed tracing, performance metrics, immutable audit records, and operational dashboards provide complete visibility into both system behavior and business operations. By separating technical observability from business intelligence, Strus gains the ability to maintain high reliability while continuously improving based on measurable data.

1. Purpose

Deliverables are the most valuable outputs of a professional project.

Unlike chat messages or comments, deliverables represent contractual work and therefore require a structured storage architecture rather than simple file uploads.

The objective of this document is to define how Strus stores, manages, versions, retrieves, and eventually removes project deliverables while preserving complete business history and minimizing storage costs.

2. Core Philosophy

Strus does not store files.

Strus stores business records.

Cloudinary stores files.

PostgreSQL stores metadata.

Business logic references metadata.

This separation allows:

inexpensive scaling
provider migration
auditability
temporary storage
permanent business history

33. Definition of a Well-Designed Storage System

A well-designed artifact system should:

Separate storage from business data.
Preserve complete submission history.
Keep files immutable after submission.
Support revisions through new submission versions.
Minimize long-term storage costs.
Scale independently of the application.
Allow future AI analysis without changing business entities.
Maintain strong access control and auditability.

Summary

The Strus storage architecture separates business ownership from physical storage. Cloudinary is responsible only for hosting files, while PostgreSQL permanently stores the business metadata that defines contractual submissions. Deliverables are grouped into structured submissions, revisions create immutable submission versions, and expired files are automatically removed after the retention period while preserving audit history. This approach minimizes infrastructure costs, simplifies future provider migration, and prepares the platform for advanced features such as AI analysis without altering the underlying data model.

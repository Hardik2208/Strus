1. Purpose

The financial system is the backbone of trust within Strus.

A project may survive delayed communication or missed deadlines, but inconsistent financial records destroy platform credibility immediately.

The payment architecture must guarantee:

Financial correctness
Complete auditability
Idempotent payment processing
Transaction traceability
Escrow safety
Accurate refunds
Immutable financial history

Money is treated differently from every other business entity.

Financial records are never rewritten.

2. Financial Philosophy

Strus is not a payment gateway.

Strus is an escrow orchestration platform.

Responsibilities:

Collect milestone funds
Hold payment state
Release payments
Process refunds
Record every transaction
Maintain financial history

Never calculate balances from mutable values.

Always derive them from immutable transactions.

33. Definition of a Well-Designed Financial System

A financial system should:

Guarantee transactional consistency.
Preserve complete financial history.
Survive retries and duplicate requests.
Separate gateway operations from business state.
Support multiple payment providers.
Maintain precise monetary calculations.
Provide complete traceability for audits.
Scale without redesign.
Summary

The Strus financial architecture treats money as immutable business data. Every payment, refund, settlement, and fee is represented by append-only financial transactions, while the payment gateway remains abstracted behind provider interfaces. Business state is separated from gateway state, ensuring that financial workflows remain deterministic, auditable, and resilient to failures. This design supports escrow-based milestone payments today while preparing the platform for future multi-provider and multi-currency expansion.

1. Purpose

The Service Layer is the heart of the backend.

Controllers should only translate HTTP requests into service calls.

Repositories should only communicate with PostgreSQL.

The Service Layer is the only place where business decisions are made.

If business logic is scattered across controllers, repositories, middleware, or utilities, the backend becomes impossible to maintain.

Every business rule defined in Strus must eventually be implemented inside the Service Layer.

22. Golden Rules
One service = one business action.
Controllers are translators, not decision-makers.
Repositories only persist data.
Business rules live only in services.
Services communicate through interfaces.
Transactions are owned by the initiating service.
Events are published after commits.
Services are transport-independent.
Services are framework-independent.
Every service is independently testable.
23. Definition of a Well-Designed Service

A service is considered complete when:

It performs one business operation.
It enforces all relevant business rules.
It coordinates repositories cleanly.
It owns its transaction.
It emits the correct domain events.
It is deterministic and idempotent where required.
It can be tested in isolation.
It has no knowledge of HTTP or database implementation details.

1. Purpose

The Reputation Domain is responsible for measuring trust across the platform.

Unlike traditional freelance marketplaces that rely solely on user ratings, Strus calculates reputation using objective business behavior recorded throughout the project lifecycle.

Reputation influences future trust but does not affect contractual obligations.

The Reputation Domain manages:

Professional reputation
Client reputation
Performance records
Score calculations
Reputation history
Score recalculation
2. Philosophy

One of the most important business rules we finalized was:

Every user has two independent reputations.

A user may be an excellent freelancer but a poor client.

Likewise,

a professional company may hire freelancers fairly while delivering poor-quality work when acting as a contractor.

Therefore reputation is role-dependent.

20. Business Rules
Every user owns two independent reputations.
Reputation is role-specific.
Performance Records are immutable.
Reputation is always calculated.
Manual score editing is prohibited.
Score changes are historically preserved.
New algorithms create new calculations.
Reputation never directly affects contract execution.
Performance metrics originate from other domains.
21. Golden Rules
Reputation is earned through behavior.
Performance Records are the source of truth.
Scores are derived, never authored.
Client and Professional reputations are independent.
Every score change is explainable.
Every calculation is reproducible.
Historical reputation is preserved forever.
Algorithm improvements never destroy history.
Trust is objective.
Reputation belongs to users, not organizations.
🚨 Architecture Observation (Important)

While reviewing all previous domains, I found one final improvement that perfectly aligns with your original vision.

Earlier we modeled reputation as global per user.

However, your platform focuses heavily on execution quality over time.

I recommend introducing:

PerformanceRecord
        │
        └── ProjectPerformanceSnapshot

Each completed project generates one immutable snapshot containing:

Client score contribution
Professional score contribution
Milestone completion rate
Delay statistics
Revision statistics
Payment behavior
Dispute outcome
Overall project health

Then:

ProjectPerformanceSnapshot

↓

Performance Records

↓

Score Calculation

↓

Reputation

This gives you:

Rich user profiles
Portfolio analytics
Future AI insights
Transparent score explanations
Historical performance trends

without changing the reputation algorithm itself.

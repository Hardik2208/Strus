1. Purpose

The Organization Domain exists to provide administrative structure for projects.

Unlike collaboration platforms such as Jira, Notion, or Linear, organizations in Strus are not collaboration workspaces.

Organizations are administrative containers.

Their responsibilities are:

Group projects
Provide administrative visibility
Maintain organizational identity
Manage project creation permissions
Separate personal work from business work

Organizations do not own people.

Projects do.

2. Organization Philosophy

One of the most important business decisions made during architecture was:

Project participants are NOT organization members.

This fundamentally differentiates Strus from project management software.

Example:

ABC Technologies
│
├── Admin: Alice
├── Admin: Bob
│
└── Project
      │
      ├── Client
      ├── Freelancer A
      ├── Freelancer B
      └── Freelancer C

The freelancers are participants in the project, not members of the organization.

Organization admins can see:

All organization projects
Organization financial summaries
Organization activity
Organization analytics

They cannot automatically perform project actions.

Project permissions remain separate.

19. Business Rules
Every project belongs to exactly one organization.
Every user automatically owns one Personal Organization.
Professional Organizations are created explicitly.
Organizations contain administrators, not members.
Project participants are independent of organization administrators.
Organization invitations are only for administrators.
Organization activity is different from project timeline.
One organization may execute multiple projects simultaneously.
Organization deletion is always soft deletion.
20. Golden Rules
Organizations are administrative containers.
Projects own execution.
Organizations never own project participants.
Administrators are not collaborators by default.
Organization visibility never overrides project permissions.
Activity and Timeline are different concepts.
Personal and Professional organizations share the same architecture.
Every project must belong to one organization.
Organization settings remain isolated from organization identity.
Administrative structure is independent of contractual relationships.

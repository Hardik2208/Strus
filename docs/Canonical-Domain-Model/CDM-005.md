1. Purpose

The Execution Domain transforms a signed agreement into executable work.

The Project defines what has been agreed.

The Execution Domain defines how the agreed work is executed.

Its responsibilities include:

Work planning
Freelancer assignments
Milestone sequencing
Deadline management
Revision management
Contract amendments
Approval tracking
Mutual termination
Execution history

It does not own payments or deliverables.

2. Philosophy

One of the most important architectural decisions we made was:

Milestones belong to a freelancer, not directly to the project.

25. Business Rules
Every project has one active WorkPlan.
Every structural modification creates a new WorkPlanVersion.
Assignments belong to WorkPlanVersions.
Milestones belong to Assignments.
Professionals progress sequentially through their own milestones.
Different professionals execute independently.
Deadlines are extended through history records.
Revisions always precede rejection.
Revision discussions occur in dedicated conversations.
Approval history is immutable.
Mutual termination requires explicit approvals.
26. Golden Rules
Execution is separate from contracts.
WorkPlans are versioned, never overwritten.
Assignments belong to WorkPlanVersions.
Milestones belong to professionals, not projects.
Deadline history is immutable.
Revision is preferred over rejection.
Conversations are contextual.
Every approval is explicitly recorded.
Every execution decision is auditable.
Execution history is never lost.
Architecture Observation

While finalizing this domain, I found one more entity that should exist to fully support your business rules:

MilestoneProgressSnapshot

You mentioned earlier that you want to retain milestone execution history, including:

Completion percentage
Effective deadline
Delay status
Extension count
Submission count
Revision count

Rather than recalculating historical values from many tables, I recommend an immutable snapshot entity:

Milestone
    │
    └── MilestoneProgressSnapshot

Each significant milestone event (submission, approval, revision, extension, completion) creates a snapshot.

This provides:

Timeline playback
Analytics
Reputation calculations
Audit history
Future reporting

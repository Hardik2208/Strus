1. Purpose

The Infrastructure Domain contains technical entities that support the business domains without owning business logic.

These entities exist to provide:

Auditability
Reliability
Scalability
Recoverability
Background processing
Event-driven communication

Unlike other domains, these entities are never manipulated directly by users.

2. Philosophy

One of the core architectural principles of Strus is:

Business logic should remain independent from infrastructure.

A Project should never know:

How notifications are sent
How events are processed
How retries work
How background workers operate

Infrastructure exists to support business domains—not replace them.

20. Business Rules
Every business action generates an audit log.
Events are published through the Outbox Pattern.
Background jobs execute asynchronously.
Infrastructure never owns business logic.
Retries are automatic.
Failed jobs are observable.
Storage cleanup is automated.
Reputation recalculation runs in the background.
Webhooks never bypass business services.
Global configuration remains centralized.
21. Golden Rules
Infrastructure supports business domains.
Audit history is immutable.
Events are durable.
Background work is asynchronous.
Retries are automatic.
Configuration is centralized.
External integrations are isolated.
Every infrastructure action is observable.
No business logic lives inside workers.
Reliability is achieved through events, not synchronous dependencies.
🚨 Architecture Observation (Final Improvement)

After reviewing all previous ten domains, I found one final architectural refinement before the Canonical Domain Model is frozen.

Instead of treating all background jobs identically, introduce:

JobQueue
    │
    └── BackgroundJob

Example queues:

Email Queue

Notification Queue

Storage Queue

Analytics Queue

Reputation Queue

Webhook Queue

This allows independent scaling later:

Storage Workers × 2

Notification Workers × 10

Analytics Workers × 1

without changing the business model.

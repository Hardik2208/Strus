1. Purpose

The Dispute Domain exists to resolve contractual disagreements without destroying project history.

A dispute is not a chat.

A dispute is not a rejection.

A dispute is a formal business process.

Its responsibilities include:

Raising disputes
Evidence collection
Discussion
Resolution
Reputation impact
Financial consequences
Project state management
2. Philosophy

One of the most important business rules we finalized was:

22. Business Rules
A dispute can only be raised after a rejection or a business disagreement requiring formal resolution.
Opening a dispute immediately places the project in ON_HOLD.
Disputes own their own conversation.
Evidence references existing artifacts.
Every dispute has one final resolution.
Resolution never directly changes financial balances.
Resolution never directly edits reputation.
Resolution generates business actions.
Performance Records determine score changes.
23. Golden Rules
Disputes are exceptional.
Evidence is reusable.
Conversations are isolated.
History is immutable.
Resolution creates actions.
Reputation is calculated.
Money is moved by the Financial Domain.
Execution resumes only after resolution.
Every dispute is permanently auditable.
Dispute logic never bypasses other domain boundaries.
🚨 Architecture Observation (Important)

While finalizing this domain, I found one improvement that aligns perfectly with your business rules.

Earlier we modeled:

Dispute

↓

Resolution

However, your workflow may involve:

Platform moderator
Organization owner
Mutual agreement
Future arbitration service

Instead of storing a single resolvedBy, introduce:

DisputeResolution
        │
        └── ResolutionParticipant

Example:

DisputeResolution
        │
        ├── Moderator Decision
        ├── Client Confirmation
        └── Professional Confirmation

This supports:

Mutual settlements
Multi-step approvals
Enterprise arbitration
Future mediator workflows

without changing the domain model later.

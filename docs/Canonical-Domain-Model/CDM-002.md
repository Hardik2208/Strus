1. Purpose

The Identity Domain represents every permanent identity that exists inside Strus.

Unlike authentication systems that only manage login credentials, the Identity Domain manages the complete lifecycle of a platform user, including authentication, verification, profile information, sessions, devices, OAuth providers, and long-term reputation.

A user remains the same individual regardless of whether they act as a client, freelancer, organization owner, or administrator.

Identity is permanent.

Roles are contextual.

2. Philosophy

A person has exactly one account.

That account can simultaneously:

Own organizations
Hire professionals
Work as a freelancer
Become an organization administrator
Participate in multiple projects

Never create multiple accounts to represent different roles.

Instead, roles emerge from participation in different business contexts.

Reputation processing entities such as PerformanceRecord, ReputationCalculation, and ReputationAdjustment belong to the Reputation Domain and are referenced by the Identity Domain rather than owned by it.

20. Business Rules
One person owns one account.
One account may play multiple business roles.
Authentication and verification are independent.
Email verification is mandatory before activation.
Phone verification is a future verification level.
KYC is independent of authentication.
Reputation is role-specific.
Reputation is calculated, never manually edited.
Every score displayed in the Identity Domain must originate from the Reputation Domain. Reputation calculations remain fully auditable through immutable Performance Records, Reputation Calculations, and Reputation Adjustments.
Sessions and devices are separate concepts.
21. Golden Rules
User is the aggregate root.
Authentication never depends on profile information.
Verification measures trust, not identity.
Reputation is bilateral.
Performance records are immutable.
Scores are derived.
Devices represent hardware.
Sessions represent logins.
OAuth providers are extensions of identity.
Identity is permanent; roles are contextual.
Current reputation entities are read models.
The Identity Domain never calculates reputation.
Reputation processing belongs exclusively to the Reputation Domain.

1. Purpose

Communication is central to every professional engagement.

Instead of embedding chat inside multiple business entities, Strus models communication as an independent domain.

This allows every business workflow to attach contextual conversations without duplicating messaging logic.

The Communication Domain manages:

Project conversations
Revision discussions
Dispute discussions
System messages
Attachments
Read receipts
Notifications
Timeline events
Activity events
2. Philosophy

One of the biggest architectural decisions was:

Communication belongs to a Conversation.

Not

Project.

Not

Revision.

Not

Dispute.

Every workflow simply owns one or more conversations.

24. Business Rules
Every discussion belongs to a Conversation.
Conversations are contextual through referenceType and referenceId.
Revision discussions are isolated from project discussions.
Dispute discussions are isolated from revisions.
Chat attachments reuse the Artifact system.
System messages are immutable.
Notifications are separate from conversations.
Timeline stores historical events.
Activity stores live operational events.
Approval decisions are synchronized through WebSockets.
25. Golden Rules
Communication is independent of business workflows.
Every workflow owns conversations instead of embedding chat.
Notifications are not messages.
Timeline is immutable history.
Activity is operational visibility.
Attachments reuse the submission storage architecture.
WebSockets synchronize state, not business logic.
Conversations remain contextual through references.
Every communication event is auditable.
The Communication Domain never owns business decisions—it only records and distributes them.
🚨 Architecture Observation

While designing this domain, I found another improvement that perfectly matches your earlier business rules.

Earlier we modeled:

Conversation
    │
    ▼
Message

However, your business rules include approval requests, termination requests, deadline extension requests, and revision requests, where users must explicitly accept or reject.

Instead of encoding these as normal messages, introduce a dedicated entity:

ActionRequest
Conversation
    │
    ├── Message
    └── ActionRequest
            ├── actionType
            ├── referenceType
            ├── referenceId
            ├── requestedBy
            ├── expiresAt
            └── status

Examples:

Contract approval request
Deadline extension approval
Mutual termination approval
Agreement amendment review
Milestone approval request

Once one contractor acts (as per your business rule), the ActionRequest is completed, and a WebSocket event immediately removes the pending action from every other participant's UI.

This models actionable workflows much more cleanly than treating them as ordinary chat messages, while still allowing the conversation to provide the surrounding discussion. I recommend adding ActionRequest as a first-class entity in the final canonical ER model.
